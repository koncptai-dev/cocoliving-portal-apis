Student Resident License Agreement

This Student Resident License Agreement (“Agreement”) is made and executed on the date specified in Annexure B.

BY AND BETWEEN:

COLLAB COLONY PRIVATE LIMITED, having its registered office at 9th Floor, 904-905, Palak Prima, Opposite Double Tree by Hilton Hotel, Iscon Ambli Road, Ahmedabad, Gujarat- 380058 and PAN No. AAMCC5937H (hereinafter referred to as the “Licensor”, which expression shall, unless repugnant to the context, include its successors and permitted assigns);

AND

The Student or Parent / Legal Guardian of the Student Resident, whose particulars are set out in Annexure A, executing this Agreement for and on behalf of the Student Resident and also in his/her personal capacity as surety/guarantor (hereinafter referred to as the “Guardian”, which expression shall, unless repugnant to the context, include his/her heirs, executors, administrators and permitted assigns).

The Licensor and Student or Parent / Guardian shall hereinafter collectively be referred to as the “Parties”. The Student Resident shall be bound by the conduct, occupancy, usage and compliance obligations under this Agreement through the Guardian and, wherever applicable, by his/her own acknowledgement. The Guardian shall remain fully liable for ensuring compliance by the Student Resident.

Annexures and Individual Particulars

The Parties agree that all individual-specific particulars relating to the Student Resident, Guardian, accommodation, bed/room allotment, commercial terms, security deposit, inventory, access devices, special conditions, and any other resident-specific details shall be recorded in the Annexures forming part of this Agreement.

The Annexures shall be deemed to be an integral part of this Agreement and shall be read together with the terms and conditions contained herein.

In the event of any inconsistency between the body of this Agreement and the Annexures, the body of this Agreement shall prevail, unless the relevant Annexure expressly records that a specific variation has been approved in writing by the Licensor.

1. Nature of Arrangement

This Agreement constitutes a leave and license arrangement and does not create any tenancy, lease, ownership, easement, exclusive possession, or any right, title or interest in favour of the Student Resident or the Guardian.

The Student Resident is permitted to occupy the allotted bed/space strictly on a limited, revocable, non-exclusive, non-transferable and conditional basis, subject to the terms of this Agreement and the operational policies of the Licensor.

The Licensor shall at all times retain overall possession, supervision, dominion and control over the Premises, including the allotted bed/room and common areas.

2. Definitions

For the purposes of this Agreement, the expression “Student Resident” shall mean the student/scholar whose particulars are set out in Annexure A, for whose stay and conduct the Guardian executes this Agreement.

The expression “Guardian” shall mean the parent/legal guardian/sponsor whose particulars are set out in Annexure A, who executes this Agreement on behalf of the Student Resident and also in his/her personal capacity as surety/guarantor for due performance of all obligations under this Agreement.

The expression “Premises” shall mean the managed co-living facility of the Licensor, the details whereof are set out in Annexure B.

3. Execution by Guardian and Surety Obligation

The Guardian confirms that he/she is legally competent and duly authorized to execute this Agreement on behalf of the Student Resident and to bind himself/herself as surety/guarantor for all obligations arising under this Agreement.

The Guardian shall be jointly and severally liable with the Student Resident for all rent/license fee, security deposit, electricity charges, penalties, damages, losses, notice period dues, lock-in dues, unauthorized occupancy charges, legal costs, indemnities and all other amounts payable under this Agreement.

The Guardian shall ensure that the Student Resident complies with the terms of this Agreement, the house rules, code of conduct, safety requirements and all directions issued by the Licensor or its authorized representatives from time to time.

Irrespective of whether the Student Resident is a minor or major, a copy of this Agreement, including its annexures, house rules and applicable policies, may be shared by the Licensor with the parent/legal guardian/emergency contact of the Student Resident. The Guardian and/or Student Resident, as applicable, consent to such sharing for information, record, compliance, communication and emergency purposes.

4. Accommodation

The Licensor permits the Student Resident to occupy accommodation on a sharing basis at the Premises described in Annexure B.

The room category, bed number, accommodation type and other allocation particulars shall be as set out in Annexure B.

The allotment of any bed, room or space is temporary and non-exclusive. The Licensor may reassign, relocate, shift or alter the accommodation arrangement for operational, administrative, safety, maintenance, disciplinary or business reasons.

5. Term and Lock-in Period

The term of stay shall be as set out in Annexure B, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Agreement.

The minimum mandatory stay/lock-in period for Student Residents if he or she is onboarded through affiliated Organization or University/ Education institute shall be equal to the total agreement period as specified in Annexure B.

The minimum mandatory stay/lock-in period for Student Residents if he or she is not onboarded through affiliated Organization or University/ Education institute shall be three (3) months.

Any exit, abandonment or discontinuation of stay before completion of the lock-in period shall constitute breach of this Agreement and shall attract the consequences specified herein.

6. Academic Conduct and Enrolment

The Student Resident shall maintain active enrolment with the educational institution disclosed at the time of onboarding and as recorded in Annexure A.

The Guardian and/or Student Resident, as applicable, shall promptly notify the Licensor of any suspension, expulsion, transfer, discontinuation, completion of course, prolonged absence from academic program, change of institution, change of course, or any other material change in academic status.

The Licensor shall be entitled to call upon the Guardian and/or Student Resident to furnish proof of active enrolment, student identity card, admission letter, fee receipt, bonafide certificate, or such other academic verification documents as may be reasonably required from time to time.

Failure to disclose any material change in academic status or failure to furnish verification documents may be treated as breach of this Agreement.

7. KYC, Academic Verification and Police Verification

The Guardian and Student Resident shall furnish all KYC documents, photographs, proof of identity, proof of address, educational institution identification, admission/enrolment documents, emergency contact details, and such other documents as may reasonably be required by the Licensor for onboarding, verification, safety, operational, regulatory, or compliance purposes.

The Guardian and Student Resident shall fully cooperate with the Licensor for police verification, statutory verification, background verification, or any other verification required under applicable law or the internal policies of the Licensor.

Failure to furnish such documents or cooperate with verification requirements may be treated as breach of this Agreement and may entitle the Licensor to withhold onboarding, suspend access, terminate occupancy, or take such other action as may be deemed appropriate.

8. Rent, Security Deposit and Payment Terms

The Guardian shall ensure payment of the monthly rent/license fee set out in Annexure B on or before the 7th day of each calendar month.

Delay in payment shall attract late payment charges of Rs. 100/- per day, without prejudice to the Licensor’s right to suspend services, restrict access, terminate this Agreement and/or recover dues.

For Student Residents, the security deposit shall ordinarily be equivalent to two (2) months’ rent/license fee or as specified in Annexure B, together with one (1) month advance rent, unless otherwise specified in Annexure B.

The security deposit shall be interest-free and shall not be adjusted against the last month’s rent or any running dues during the subsistence of this Agreement.

The security deposit shall be refunded separately after vacating and completion of exit formalities, subject to adjustment of unpaid rent/license fee, electricity charges, notice period dues, penalties, damages, missing items, breach-related losses, unauthorized occupancy charges and all other recoverable amounts.

Subject to completion of exit formalities and adjustment of all recoverable dues, the security deposit shall ordinarily be refunded within fifteen (15) days from the date of vacating.

In case of breach, misconduct, illegal activity, unauthorized exit, premature termination contrary to this Agreement, or non-compliance with exit formalities, the Licensor shall be entitled to forfeit the security deposit wholly or partly.

The rent/license fee shall be subject to annual increment of five percent (5%), unless otherwise specified in Annexure B.

9. Services and Inclusions

Subject to operational feasibility, availability and internal policies, the rent/license fee may include the services and facilities specified in Annexure B, including bed, mattress, cupboard, fan/AC, meals, Wi-Fi, housekeeping, linen change, laundry, power backup, CCTV in common areas, community activities and career support, as applicable.

The Licensor may modify, suspend, discontinue, substitute or regulate any service, facility, amenity, timing or operational process due to operational, maintenance, technical, staffing, safety, legal or business reasons.

10. Electricity

Electricity consumption within the allotted room shall be charged at the rate specified in Annexure B, presently Rs. {{electricity_rate}} per unit, or such revised rate as notified by the Licensor.

Electricity consumption in common areas shall ordinarily be included in the rent/license fee, unless otherwise specified.

11. Prohibited Appliances

Electric kettles, induction cooktops, heaters, hot plates, immersion rods, gas stoves, electric cookers, and any other cooking, heating, or high-load electrical appliances shall not be used inside rooms unless expressly permitted by the Licensor in writing.

Any unauthorized use of such appliances shall constitute breach of this Agreement. The Guardian and/or Student Resident shall be liable for any penalty, damage, fire hazard, electrical fault, increased electricity consumption, injury, loss, or liability arising out of such unauthorized use.

12. Meal Policy

Meals shall be provided as per the timings and meal plan prescribed by the Licensor from time to time.

Meal preferences such as Jain or Swaminarayan meals must be selected at onboarding and shall ordinarily not be changed thereafter.

The cafeteria air-conditioning shall ordinarily operate thirty (30) minutes prior to meal timings.

13. Laundry Policy

Laundry services shall be limited to three (3) items per day, excluding undergarments, unless otherwise permitted by the Licensor.

Expensive, delicate or premium items shall be given at the Student Resident’s and Guardian’s own risk. Compensation for loss or damage, if attributable to the laundry process, shall be capped at Rs. 500/- per item.

Premium laundry services may be availed at additional charges.

14. Property Use and Damage

The Student Resident shall maintain the room, bed, furniture, fixtures, appliances, common areas and all facilities in proper condition.

Any damage, loss, breakage, misuse, alteration or unauthorized use caused by the Student Resident or his/her visitors shall be recoverable from the Guardian and/or adjusted from the security deposit.

The Student Resident shall not affix posters, stickers, nails, adhesives, hooks or any material on walls, cupboards, furniture or fixtures, and shall not alter, repair, tamper with or modify any part of the Premises.

15. Access, Inspection and Management Control

The Licensor, its staff, representatives, maintenance personnel, security, housekeeping and authorized agents shall have the right to access the allotted room/bed and common areas for inspection, maintenance, housekeeping, repair, safety checks, verification, emergencies and operational purposes.

No separate consent shall be required for access in case of emergency, safety concern, suspected breach, maintenance requirement or operational necessity.

16. Access Card / Biometric / Key Misuse

Access cards, biometric credentials, QR codes, mobile access passes, keys, or any other access devices shall be used only by the authorized Student Resident to whom they are issued.

The Student Resident shall not share, duplicate, transfer, lend, misuse, tamper with, or permit any third party to use such access cards, biometric credentials, QR codes, mobile access passes, keys or access devices.

Any loss, misuse, unauthorized sharing, duplication, tampering or transfer of access devices shall be immediately reported to the Licensor and shall attract replacement charges, penalties, disciplinary action, and/or termination, as may be determined by the Licensor.

The Guardian shall remain liable for any loss, damage, misuse, unauthorized access, security breach, or consequential liability arising out of breach of this clause by the Student Resident.

17. Safety and Personal Belongings

The Student Resident shall be solely responsible for safeguarding personal belongings and is advised not to keep valuables, cash, jewellery, expensive electronics or important documents unattended.

The Licensor shall not be liable for loss, theft, misplacement, disappearance or damage to personal belongings, whether caused by other residents, visitors, staff, third parties, accidents or unforeseen circumstances.

18. CCTV Surveillance and Common Area Privacy

The Guardian and Student Resident acknowledge that CCTV surveillance may operate in common areas of the Premises for safety, security, operational monitoring, incident verification, and protection of residents, staff and property.

No CCTV surveillance shall be installed in private areas such as washrooms or any other area where privacy is legally protected.

The Guardian and Student Resident agree that no expectation of privacy shall exist in common areas, including entrances, corridors, reception areas, cafeteria, lounges, parking areas, staircases, access points and other shared spaces.

19. Medical Emergency

In the event of any medical emergency, illness, accident, injury, or health-related concern involving the Student Resident, the Licensor may, without being under any obligation to do so, arrange immediate medical assistance, ambulance support, hospitalization, transportation, or other emergency support as may be reasonably considered necessary.

The Licensor shall make reasonable efforts to inform the Guardian/emergency contact at the earliest practicable opportunity. All costs, expenses, charges, medical bills, transportation costs, hospital charges, and incidental expenses incurred in relation to such medical assistance shall be borne by the Guardian and/or Student Resident.

The Licensor shall not be liable for any consequence arising out of delay, non-availability of medical services, treatment decisions, medical advice, or acts/omissions of hospitals, doctors, ambulance providers, or third-party medical service providers.

20. Roommate Compatibility

The Guardian and Student Resident acknowledge that the Premises operate as a shared co-living facility and that the Licensor does not guarantee compatibility, personal comfort, habits, lifestyle, food preferences, study patterns, sleep schedules, or interpersonal adjustment between residents sharing accommodation.

Any request for room change, bed change, roommate change, or relocation shall be subject to availability, operational feasibility, payment of applicable charges, and the sole discretion of the Licensor.

Interpersonal disagreements or lack of compatibility shall not by itself entitle the Student Resident or Guardian to terminate this Agreement, claim refund, waive lock-in obligations, or avoid payment obligations.

21. Visitors and Access

Visitor access shall be subject to the policies of the Licensor. Overnight stay of visitors is strictly prohibited.

Late entry after 11:00 PM and night-outs shall be intimated in advance through the communication mode prescribed by the Licensor.

Female visitors may be permitted until 8:00 PM subject to prior approval of management. The café/common café facility may remain operational until 1:00 AM, subject to change.

The Licensor may regulate, restrict, deny, record or monitor visitor access in the interest of safety, discipline, reputation, privacy and operations.

22. Deliveries and Parcels

Food deliveries and parcels shall be collected by the Student Resident from the designated reception/collection area.

Parcels must ordinarily be collected on the same day of delivery. Delay in collection shall be communicated to the warden/management.

The Licensor shall not be responsible for loss, theft, deterioration, damage, delay or misplacement of deliveries or parcels.

23. Food, Pantry and Hygiene

All food items stored in common areas or refrigerators shall be labelled with the Student Resident’s name and room number.

Unlabelled, expired, spoiled, open, leaking or abandoned food items may be discarded by management without liability.

Sanitary pads and sanitary waste shall not be flushed and must be disposed of only in designated bins.

24. Infestation and Pest Control

The Student Resident shall maintain the allotted room, bed space, storage area, food items, personal belongings, and common areas used by him/her in a clean, hygienic, and pest-free condition.

Where any pest infestation, contamination, foul smell, unhygienic condition, damage, or remediation requirement is attributable to the acts, omissions, negligence, storage practices, food habits, waste disposal, or breach of hygiene obligations by the Student Resident, the cost of treatment, pest control, deep cleaning, remediation, repair, replacement, and allied expenses may be recovered from the Guardian and/or adjusted against the security deposit.

25. Staff Interaction

The Student Resident shall not offer or provide gifts, cash, gratuities, tips, loans, favours or inducements to housekeeping, security, kitchen, maintenance or other staff members.

26. Complaints and Support

All complaints and service requests shall be raised through the designated mobile application or such mode as may be notified by the Licensor.

The warden shall ordinarily be the first point of contact. Resolution timelines shall be governed by the Licensor’s policies and operational feasibility.

27. Health, Safety and Medical Disclosure

The Guardian shall disclose any pre-existing medical condition, allergy, psychological condition, contagious disease or relevant health concern of the Student Resident prior to onboarding.

In case of fire or emergency, the Student Resident shall use stairs and not lifts.

All electrical appliances, lights, fans, AC units and fittings shall be switched off before leaving the room.

The Licensor may take immediate precautionary measures, including isolation, medical referral, guardian intimation, relocation or restriction of access, where required for health, hygiene, safety or security.

28. Internet / Wi-Fi Usage

Internet and Wi-Fi access is provided subject to fair usage norms, network availability, technical feasibility, and the policies of the Licensor.

The Student Resident shall not use the internet facility for unlawful activities, downloading, accessing, storing, circulating or transmitting prohibited, obscene, pirated or illegal content, hacking, phishing, cyber offences, online harassment, copyright infringement, piracy, gambling, cryptocurrency mining, bulk downloads, server hosting, network abuse, or any activity detrimental to network security, other residents, or the reputation of the Licensor.

The Licensor may restrict, suspend, monitor, regulate, or disable internet access in case of suspected misuse, excessive usage, network abuse, legal compliance requirements, security concerns, or operational necessity.

The Guardian shall remain liable for any loss, claim, notice, penalty, investigation, or proceeding arising out of misuse of internet services by the Student Resident.

29. General Guidelines and Substance Prohibition

Possession, consumption, storage, distribution, sale, supply, sharing, or use of alcoholic beverages, smoking materials, vaping devices, tobacco products, narcotic substances, psychotropic substances, prohibited substances, or any other similar intoxicating or restricted substance within the Premises is strictly prohibited. (any sort of prohibited activities as per law)

Any breach of this clause shall be treated as serious misconduct and may result in immediate termination, eviction without refund, forfeiture of deposit, intimation to Guardian, police complaint, and/or other legal action.

All policies, updates, house rules, circulars and communications conveyed through mobile application, WhatsApp groups, email, on-premise displays or other digital platforms shall be binding.

30. Code of Conduct and Community Standards

The Student Resident acknowledges that the Premises operate as a shared co-living environment and shall maintain conduct ensuring safety, discipline, hygiene, mutual respect and peaceful co-existence.

The Student Resident shall not engage in any act, omission or behaviour that disrupts peaceful enjoyment, interferes with operations, causes nuisance or affects the safety, reputation or discipline of the Premises.

31. Anti-Ragging, Anti-Bullying and Anti-Harassment

Ragging, bullying, harassment, discrimination, intimidation, stalking, assault, threats, coercion, or any form of physical, verbal, emotional, sexual, online, digital or psychological abuse is strictly prohibited within the Premises and in relation to any resident, staff member, visitor or representative of the Licensor.

The Student Resident shall not engage in any conduct, whether inside or outside the Premises, which affects the safety, dignity, privacy, reputation, mental well-being or peaceful occupation of any other resident, staff member or visitor.

Any violation of this clause may result in immediate disciplinary action, including warning, restriction of access, intimation to Guardian, relocation, suspension of services, termination of occupancy, eviction without refund, forfeiture of deposit, police complaint and/or any other legal action as may be necessary.

The decision of the Licensor in matters concerning safety, discipline, anti-ragging, anti-bullying and anti-harassment shall be final and binding, subject to applicable law.

32. Photography, Recording and Social Media Conduct

The Student Resident shall not photograph, video record, audio record, livestream, capture images, or otherwise record other residents, staff, visitors, restricted areas, security arrangements, internal operations, or private/common areas of the Premises without consent or authorization, except where required by law.

The Student Resident shall not publish, circulate, forward, upload or share false, malicious, defamatory, misleading, abusive, obscene, unlawful, or reputation-damaging statements, images, videos, posts, reviews or content regarding the Premises, Licensor, management, residents, staff, services, facilities or operations through social media, messaging platforms, public forums, online reviews or any other public/private digital medium.

Nothing contained herein shall restrict the Guardian or Student Resident from raising genuine grievances through lawful and appropriate channels.

Any breach of this clause shall be treated as misconduct and may invite disciplinary and/or legal action.

33. Misconduct

Misconduct shall include, without limitation: abusive, threatening, violent, offensive or inappropriate behaviour; ragging; bullying; harassment; discrimination; stalking; illegal activities; possession, consumption or distribution of prohibited substances; repeated violation of house rules; nuisance or disturbance; property damage; unauthorized occupancy or guests; non-compliance with management instructions; internet misuse; access device misuse; and any act affecting safety, reputation, discipline or operations.

34. Consequences of Misconduct

The Licensor may take action including warning, penalty, suspension of services, restriction of access, relocation, intimation to Guardian, termination, eviction without refund, forfeiture of deposit and/or legal action.

The decision of the Licensor in matters of safety, discipline, conduct, relocation and operational control shall be final and binding.

35. Social Living Risks

The Guardian and Student Resident acknowledge the inherent risks and practical limitations of shared/community living.

The Licensor shall not be responsible for disputes between residents, interpersonal conflicts, emotional distress, personal disagreements or acts/omissions of other residents.

36. Indemnity

The Guardian, for himself/herself and on behalf of the Student Resident, shall indemnify, defend and hold harmless the Licensor, its directors, officers, employees, representatives and agents from all claims, losses, liabilities, damages, penalties, proceedings and expenses including reasonable legal costs arising from breach of this Agreement, misconduct, violation of law, acts or omissions of the Student Resident or visitors, third-party disputes, damage to property or breach of policies.

This clause shall survive termination or expiry of this Agreement.

37. Limitation of Liability

The Licensor shall not be liable for acts of other residents, disputes, emotional distress, service interruptions, internet downtime, theft/loss of belongings, indirect losses or force majeure events.

The aggregate liability of the Licensor, if established, shall not exceed the security deposit actually received under this Agreement.

38. Data Privacy and Consent

The Guardian and Student Resident consent to the collection, storage, processing, verification, use and retention of personal information, identity documents, academic details, guardian details, emergency contact details, medical disclosures, access records, CCTV records, payment records, complaint records, communication records and other resident-related information by the Licensor.

Such information may be used for operational, security, regulatory, statutory verification, police verification, resident management, safety, emergency, dispute resolution, compliance and record-keeping purposes.

The Licensor may disclose such information to law enforcement authorities, statutory authorities, educational institutions, verification agencies, service providers, legal advisors, emergency contacts, parents/legal guardians, or other persons where required for lawful, operational, compliance, safety or emergency purposes.

The Licensor shall process such information subject to applicable laws.

39. Evidence and Enforcement

CCTV footage, access records, complaint records, digital logs, emails, WhatsApp communications, application records, internal reports and electronic records may be relied upon as evidence.

Electronic records shall be valid and admissible for all purposes relating to enforcement of this Agreement.

40. Termination and Exit

During the lock-in period, if the Student Resident exits, vacates, abandons or discontinues stay, the Guardian shall be liable to pay rent/license fee for the balance lock-in period together with all applicable charges until such replacement is secured.

Withdrawal from, suspension by, completion of, discontinuation of, transfer from, or any other change in the Student Resident’s educational program or educational institution shall not automatically terminate this Agreement, reduce or waive the lock-in period, waive notice period obligations, waive financial obligations, or entitle the Guardian and/or Student Resident to claim refund, unless expressly approved by the Licensor in writing.

The Guardian and/or Student Resident shall continue to remain liable for all rent/license fee, notice period dues, lock-in dues, electricity charges, penalties, damages and other amounts payable under this Agreement until valid termination and completion of exit formalities in accordance with this Agreement.

If the Guardian/Student Resident identifies a suitable replacement acceptable to the Licensor and such replacement executes a fresh agreement and occupies the accommodation, liability shall stand reduced up to the date of onboarding of such replacement. Approval of replacement shall remain at the sole discretion of the Licensor.

After completion of the lock-in period, termination may be effected only by giving Thirty (30) days’ prior written notice. Failure to provide such notice shall entitle the Licensor to deduct notice period dues equivalent to Thirty (30) days’ rent/license fee from the security deposit and/or recover the same separately.

At the time of exit, the Student Resident and Guardian shall clear all outstanding dues, return all access devices/assets, hand over possession peacefully and complete all exit formalities.

Exit shall be subject to joint inspection of the allotted accommodation, inventory verification, verification of access cards, keys, biometric/access devices, assessment of electricity charges, outstanding dues, damages, missing items, penalties, notice period dues, unauthorized occupancy charges, and completion of all exit formalities prescribed by the Licensor.

In case of overstay after termination, expiry or notice period, penal unauthorized occupancy charges shall apply on a per-day basis as determined by the Licensor, and the Licensor may restrict services and take lawful steps to recover possession.

The Licensor may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect in case of breach, misconduct, non-payment, illegal activity, safety concern, policy violation or conduct prejudicial to the Premises or its occupants.

41. Abandoned Property

Any belongings, luggage, documents, parcels, personal articles, food items, electronic items, valuables, or any other property left behind by the Student Resident after exit, termination, expiry, abandonment, or vacating of the Premises shall be collected by the Guardian and/or Student Resident within seven (7) days from the date of vacating or such other date as may be notified by the Licensor.

If such belongings remain uncollected beyond seven (7) days, the Licensor shall be entitled to remove, dispose of, donate, discard, store, or otherwise deal with such belongings in such manner as the Licensor deems fit, without any liability.

Any storage, handling, disposal, transportation, administrative, or allied charges incurred by the Licensor may be recovered from the Guardian and/or adjusted against the security deposit.

42. Force Majeure

The Licensor shall not be liable for delay, interruption or failure caused by events beyond reasonable control including natural calamities, pandemics, government restrictions, utility failure, technical failure, strikes, riots, labour shortage, fire, flood or acts of God.

43. Dispute Resolution and Arbitration

The Parties shall first attempt amicable resolution. Failing amicable resolution, disputes shall be referred to arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.

The arbitral tribunal shall consist of a sole arbitrator mutually appointed by the Parties. In case of failure to agree within fifteen (15) days, the arbitrator shall be appointed in accordance with the Act.

The seat and venue of arbitration shall be Ahmedabad and the language shall be English.

44. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Agreement shall be governed by Indian law. Subject to the arbitration clause, courts at Ahmedabad alone shall have exclusive jurisdiction, notwithstanding the place of execution or residence of the Guardian or Student Resident.

45. Communication

All notices, approvals, consents and communications may be sent to the Guardian and/or Student Resident through RPAD, courier, email, mobile application, WhatsApp or other recognized digital platforms using the details specified in Annexure A.

Electronic communications shall be deemed received upon successful transmission unless a delivery failure notification is received.

46. Digital Execution and Electronic Acceptance

This Agreement may be executed physically or electronically, including through digital signatures, OTP-based acceptance, mobile application acknowledgement, email confirmation, scanned signatures, e-sign platforms, click-wrap acceptance, or any other legally recognized electronic means.

Each such mode shall constitute valid execution, acceptance, acknowledgement, and binding consent to this Agreement and the annexures forming part hereof.

The Parties agree that electronic records, digital acknowledgements, OTP logs, application records, emails, scanned copies, and electronically executed versions of this Agreement shall have the same legal effect as a physically signed document.

47. Amendments

The Licensor may amend operational policies, house rules, guidelines and procedures upon reasonable prior notice. However, amendments to core commercial terms shall require written consent, unless otherwise permitted under this Agreement.

48. Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with its annexures and documents incorporated by reference, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior discussions, representations and understandings.

49. Severability

If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

50. Waiver

No failure or delay in enforcing any right shall constitute waiver. Any waiver must be in writing and shall apply only to the specific instance for which it is granted.

51. Survival

Clauses relating to dues, damages, indemnity, limitation of liability, dispute resolution, jurisdiction, evidence and accrued rights shall survive termination or expiry.

52. Binding Effect

This Agreement shall bind the Licensor, Guardian, Student Resident and their respective successors, legal representatives, heirs and permitted assigns.

53. No Tenancy / Exclusive Possession Disclaimer

The Guardian and Student Resident expressly acknowledge that no tenancy, lease, sub-tenancy, exclusive possession or proprietary right is created under this Agreement.

The accommodation forms part of a managed co-living facility with bundled services, amenities and shared infrastructure. Occupancy is incidental to the provision of such managed services.

The Student Resident shall not claim protection under rent control laws or any statute applicable to tenants or lessees.

54. Declaration

The Guardian declares that all information furnished in the annexures is true and correct, that he/she has read and understood this Agreement, and that he/she voluntarily executes this Agreement on behalf of the Student Resident and in personal capacity as surety/guarantor.

Do’s
  1. Maintain cleanliness and hygiene in the allotted room, bed space, washrooms and common areas.
  2. Pay rent, electricity charges, penalties and other dues within prescribed timelines.
  3. Maintain active enrolment with the disclosed educational institution and inform the Licensor of any change in academic status.
  4. Use access cards, keys, biometric access, QR codes and mobile passes responsibly and only for personal access.
  5. Intimate the Licensor/warden regarding late entry, night-outs, prolonged absence or any emergency.
  6. Immediately report loss of keys, access cards, access devices, parcels or personal belongings.
  7. Raise complaints and service requests only through the designated application or prescribed communication channel.
  8. Cooperate during inspection, maintenance, police verification, statutory verification and exit formalities.
  9. Use Wi-Fi/internet facilities responsibly and only for lawful purposes.
  10. Respect other residents, staff, visitors and the shared co-living environment.
  11. Dispose of waste, sanitary products and food items only in designated bins/areas.
  12. Switch off lights, fans, AC units, chargers and electrical appliances before leaving the room.
  13. Collect parcels and deliveries promptly from the designated area.
  14. Keep food items labelled with name and room/bed number.
  15. Report any safety concern, damage, misconduct, suspicious activity, ragging, bullying, harassment or emergency immediately.
  16. Follow all house rules, operational policies, circulars and instructions issued by the Licensor.
  17. Keep the Guardian informed of all important communications, dues, warnings or notices received from the Licensor.
  18. Cooperate with the management in maintaining discipline, safety, hygiene and peaceful co-living.
Don’ts
  1. Do not smoke, vape, consume alcohol, or possess/consume prohibited substances in rooms or common areas.
  2. Do not engage in ragging, bullying, harassment, discrimination, intimidation, stalking, assault, threats or any form of abuse.
  3. Do not allow overnight visitors or unauthorized guests.
  4. Do not share, duplicate, transfer, lend or misuse access cards, keys, biometric credentials, QR codes or mobile access passes.
  5. Do not damage, alter, tamper with or misuse furniture, fittings, appliances, fixtures, CCTV, fire safety equipment or electrical systems.
  6. Do not use electric kettles, induction cooktops, heaters, hot plates, immersion rods, gas stoves or cooking/heating appliances inside rooms without written permission.
  7. Do not affix posters, stickers, nails, hooks, adhesives or any material on walls, cupboards, furniture or fixtures.
  8. Do not create nuisance, disturbance, loud noise, harassment, intimidation or inconvenience to other residents.
  9. Do not photograph, record, livestream or capture images/videos of other residents, staff, visitors, restricted areas or internal operations without consent.
  10. Do not publish false, defamatory, malicious, misleading, abusive or reputation-damaging content regarding the Premises, management, staff or residents.
  11. Do not keep valuables, cash, jewellery or important documents unattended.
  12. Do not flush sanitary pads, food waste, plastic, cloth or other prohibited materials in toilets.
  13. Do not store expired, leaking, open, spoiled or unlabelled food in common areas.
  14. Do not obstruct corridors, exits, fire passages, staircases or common areas.
  15. Do not misuse Wi-Fi/internet for unlawful activity, piracy, hacking, prohibited downloads, cyber offences or network abuse.
  16. Do not park vehicles outside designated areas.
  17. Do not offer gifts, tips, cash, loans or favours to staff.
  18. Do not leave belongings behind after exit.
  19. Do not conceal suspension, expulsion, transfer, discontinuation or change in academic status.
  20. Do not violate any instruction issued for safety, discipline, hygiene, security or operational control.
