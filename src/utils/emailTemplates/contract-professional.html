Working Professional Resident License Agreement

This Working Professional Resident License Agreement (“Agreement”) is made and executed on the date specified in Annexure B.

BY AND BETWEEN:

COLLAB COLONY PRIVATE LIMITED, having its registered office at 9th Floor, 904-905, Palak Prima, Opposite Double Tree by Hilton Hotel, Iscon Ambli Road, Ahmedabad, Gujarat- 380058 and PAN No. AAMCC5937H (hereinafter referred to as the “Licensor”, which expression shall, unless repugnant to the context, include its successors and permitted assigns);

AND

The Working Professional / Resident whose particulars are set out in Annexure A (hereinafter referred to as the “Resident”, which expression shall, unless repugnant to the context, include his/her heirs, executors, administrators and permitted assigns).

The Licensor and the Resident shall hereinafter collectively be referred to as the “Parties”.

Annexures and Individual Particulars

The Parties agree that all individual-specific particulars relating to the Resident, accommodation, bed/room allotment, commercial terms, security deposit, inventory, access devices, special conditions, and any other resident-specific details shall be recorded in the Annexures forming part of this Agreement.

The Annexures shall be deemed to be an integral part of this Agreement and shall be read together with the terms and conditions contained herein.

In the event of any inconsistency between the body of this Agreement and the Annexures, the body of this Agreement shall prevail, unless the relevant Annexure expressly records that a specific variation has been approved in writing by the Licensor.

1. Nature of Arrangement

This Agreement constitutes a leave and license arrangement and does not create any tenancy, lease, ownership, easement, exclusive possession, or any right, title or interest in favour of the Resident.

The Resident is permitted to occupy the allotted bed/space strictly on a limited, revocable, non-exclusive, non-transferable and conditional basis, subject to the terms of this Agreement and the operational policies of the Licensor.

The Licensor shall at all times retain overall possession, supervision, dominion and control over the Premises, including the allotted bed/room and common areas.

2. Definitions

The expression “Resident” shall mean the working professional whose particulars are set out in Annexure A.

The expression “Premises” shall mean the managed co-living facility of the Licensor, the details whereof are set out in Annexure B.

3. Employment and Verification

The Resident represents that he/she is a working professional and shall promptly furnish proof of employment, official identification, offer letter, employee identity card, appointment letter, professional engagement proof, or such other documents as may reasonably be required by the Licensor for verification purposes.

The Resident shall promptly notify the Licensor of any material change in employment or professional status, including resignation, termination, transfer, prolonged leave, change of employer, commencement of self-employment, or cessation of professional engagement.

The Resident shall cooperate in furnishing such documents and information as may be required for police verification, statutory verification, background verification, or any other verification required under applicable law or the Licensor’s internal policies.

4. Accommodation

The Licensor permits the Resident to occupy accommodation on a sharing basis at the Premises described in Annexure B.

The room category, bed number, accommodation type and other allocation particulars shall be as set out in Annexure B.

The allotment of any bed, room or space is temporary and non-exclusive. The Licensor may reassign, relocate, shift or alter the accommodation arrangement for operational, administrative, safety, maintenance, disciplinary or business reasons.

5. Term and Lock-in Period

The term of stay shall be as set out in Annexure B, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Agreement.

The minimum mandatory stay/lock-in period for Working Professionals shall be three (3) months, or such other period as may be specified in Annexure B.

Any exit, abandonment or discontinuation of stay before completion of the lock-in period shall constitute breach of this Agreement and shall attract the consequences specified herein.

6. Rent, Security Deposit and Payment Terms

The Resident shall pay the monthly rent/license fee set out in Annexure B on or before the 7th day of each calendar month.

Delay in payment shall attract late payment charges of Rs. 100/- per day, without prejudice to the Licensor’s right to suspend services, restrict access, terminate this Agreement and/or recover dues.

For Working Professionals, the security deposit shall ordinarily be equivalent to one (1) month’s rent/license fee, together with one (1) month advance rent, unless otherwise specified in Annexure B.

The security deposit shall be interest-free and shall not be adjusted against the last month’s rent or any running dues during the subsistence of this Agreement.

Subject to completion of exit formalities, joint inspection, verification of inventory and access devices, and adjustment of all recoverable dues, the security deposit shall ordinarily be refunded within fifteen (15) days from the date of vacating.

In case of breach, misconduct, illegal activity, unauthorized exit, premature termination contrary to this Agreement, or non-compliance with exit formalities, the Licensor shall be entitled to forfeit the security deposit wholly or partly.

The rent/license fee shall be subject to annual increment of five percent (5%), unless otherwise specified in Annexure B.

7. Services and Inclusions

Subject to operational feasibility, availability and internal policies, the rent/license fee may include the services and facilities specified in Annexure B, including bed, mattress, cupboard, fan/AC, meals, Wi-Fi, housekeeping, linen change, laundry, power backup, CCTV in common areas, community activities and career support, as applicable.

The Licensor may modify, suspend, discontinue, substitute or regulate any service, facility, amenity, timing or operational process due to operational, maintenance, technical, staffing, safety, legal or business reasons.

8. Electricity

Electricity consumption within the allotted room shall be charged at the rate specified in Annexure B, presently Rs. {{electricity_rate}} per unit, or such revised rate as notified by the Licensor.

Electricity consumption in common areas shall ordinarily be included in the rent/license fee, unless otherwise specified.

9. Prohibited Appliances

Electric kettles, induction cooktops, heaters, hot plates, immersion rods, gas stoves, electric cookers, and any other cooking, heating, or high-load electrical appliances shall not be used inside rooms unless expressly permitted by the Licensor in writing.

Any unauthorized use of such appliances shall constitute breach of this Agreement and the Resident shall be liable for any penalty, damage, fire hazard, electrical fault, increased consumption, or loss arising therefrom.

10. Meal Policy

Meals shall be provided as per the timings and meal plan prescribed by the Licensor from time to time.

Meal preferences such as Jain or Swaminarayan meals must be selected at onboarding and shall ordinarily not be changed thereafter.

The cafeteria air-conditioning shall ordinarily operate thirty (30) minutes prior to meal timings.

11. Laundry Policy

Laundry services shall be limited to three (3) items per day, excluding undergarments, unless otherwise permitted by the Licensor.

Expensive, delicate or premium items shall be given at the Resident’s own risk. Compensation for loss or damage, if attributable to the laundry process, shall be capped at Rs. 500/- per item.

Premium laundry services may be availed at additional charges.

12. Property Use and Damage

The Resident shall maintain the room, bed, furniture, fixtures, appliances, common areas and all facilities in proper condition.

Any damage, loss, breakage, misuse, alteration or unauthorized use caused by the Resident or his/her visitors shall be recoverable from the Resident and/or adjusted from the security deposit.

The Resident shall not affix posters, stickers, nails, adhesives, hooks or any material on walls, cupboards, furniture or fixtures, and shall not alter, repair, tamper with or modify any part of the Premises.

13. Access, Inspection and Management Control

The Licensor, its staff, representatives, maintenance personnel, security, housekeeping and authorised agents shall have the right to access the allotted room/bed and common areas for inspection, maintenance, housekeeping, repair, safety checks, verification, emergencies and operational purposes.

No separate consent shall be required for access in case of emergency, safety concern, suspected breach, maintenance requirement or operational necessity.

14. Access Card / Biometric / Key Misuse

Access cards, biometric credentials, QR codes, mobile access passes, keys, or similar access devices shall be used solely by the Resident to whom they are issued.

The Resident shall not share, duplicate, transfer, lend, misuse, tamper with, or permit any third party to use such access devices or credentials.

Any loss, misuse, unauthorized sharing, or duplication of access devices shall be immediately reported to the Licensor and shall attract replacement charges, penalties, and/or disciplinary action as may be determined by the Licensor.

15. Safety and Personal Belongings

The Resident shall be solely responsible for safeguarding personal belongings and is advised not to keep valuables, cash, jewellery, expensive electronics or important documents unattended.

The Licensor shall not be liable for loss, theft, misplacement, disappearance or damage to personal belongings, whether caused by other residents, visitors, staff, third parties, accidents or unforeseen circumstances.

16. CCTV Surveillance and Common Area Privacy

The Resident acknowledges that CCTV surveillance may operate in common areas of the Premises for safety, security, operational monitoring, and incident verification purposes.

No CCTV surveillance shall be installed in private areas such as washrooms or other areas where privacy is legally protected.

The Resident agrees that no expectation of privacy shall exist in common areas, including entrances, corridors, reception areas, cafeteria, lounges, parking areas, and other shared spaces.

17. Visitors and Access

Visitor access shall be subject to the policies of the Licensor. Overnight stay of visitors is strictly prohibited.

Late entry after 11:00 PM and night-outs shall be intimated in advance through the communication mode prescribed by the Licensor.

Female visitors may be permitted until 8:00 PM subject to prior approval of management. The café/common café facility may remain operational until 1:00 AM, subject to change.

The Licensor may regulate, restrict, deny, record or monitor visitor access in the interest of safety, discipline, reputation, privacy and operations.

18. Deliveries and Parcels

Food deliveries and parcels shall be collected by the Resident from the designated reception/collection area.

Parcels must ordinarily be collected on the same day of delivery. Delay in collection shall be communicated to the warden/management.

The Licensor shall not be responsible for loss, theft, deterioration, damage, delay or misplacement of deliveries or parcels.

19. Abandoned Property

Any belongings, articles, luggage, documents, parcels, or personal items left behind by the Resident after exit, termination, expiry, abandonment, or vacating of the Premises shall be collected by the Resident within seven (7) days.

If such belongings remain uncollected beyond seven (7) days, the Licensor shall be entitled to remove, dispose of, donate, discard, store, or otherwise deal with such belongings in such manner as the Licensor deems fit, without any liability.

Any storage, handling, disposal, transportation, or administrative charges incurred by the Licensor may be recovered from the Resident or adjusted from the security deposit.

20. Food, Pantry and Hygiene

All food items stored in common areas or refrigerators shall be labelled with the Resident’s name and room number.

Unlabelled, expired, spoiled, open, leaking or abandoned food items may be discarded by management without liability.

Sanitary pads and sanitary waste shall not be flushed and must be disposed of only in designated bins.

21. Staff Interaction

The Resident shall not offer or provide gifts, cash, gratuities, tips, loans, favours or inducements to housekeeping, security, kitchen, maintenance or other staff members.

22. Complaints and Support

All complaints and service requests shall be raised through the designated mobile application or such mode as may be notified by the Licensor.

The warden shall ordinarily be the first point of contact. Resolution timelines shall be governed by the Licensor’s policies and operational feasibility.

23. Health, Safety and Medical Disclosure

The Resident shall disclose any pre-existing medical condition, allergy, psychological condition, contagious disease or relevant health concern prior to onboarding.

In case of fire or emergency, the Resident shall use stairs and not lifts.

All electrical appliances, lights, fans, AC units and fittings shall be switched off before leaving the room.

The Licensor may take immediate precautionary measures in the interest of health, hygiene, safety or security.

24. Internet / Wi-Fi Usage

Internet and Wi-Fi access is provided subject to fair usage norms, network availability, technical feasibility, and policies of the Licensor.

The Resident shall not use the internet facility for unlawful activities, downloading or transmitting prohibited or obscene content, hacking, phishing, copyright infringement, piracy, cyber offences, harassment, gambling, cryptocurrency mining, bulk downloads, server hosting, or any activity detrimental to network security or the reputation of the Licensor.

The Licensor may restrict, suspend, monitor, or regulate internet access in case of suspected misuse, excessive usage, network abuse, legal compliance requirements, or operational necessity.

25. General Guidelines

Possession, consumption, storage or distribution of alcoholic beverages, smoking and vaping substances, tobacco products, narcotic substances, psychotropic substances, prohibited substances, or any other similar intoxicating or restricted substances within the Premises is strictly prohibited.

All policies, updates, house rules, circulars and communications conveyed through mobile application, WhatsApp groups, email, on-premise displays or other digital platforms shall be binding.

26. Work From Home Policy

The Premises are intended for residential use only. Work-from-home activities of an incidental nature may be undertaken by the Resident, provided that such activities do not create nuisance, disturb other residents, increase utility consumption beyond normal residential usage, involve client visits, require installation of special equipment, or amount to conducting a commercial establishment from the Premises.

The Resident shall ensure that any work-from-home activity remains limited to personal professional work and does not interfere with the residential and co-living character of the Premises.

27. Client / Business Visitor Restriction

The Resident shall not receive clients, customers, vendors, business associates, employees, agents, or any other persons for commercial meetings, business transactions, sales activities, recruitment activities, or professional consultations within the Premises without prior written approval of the Licensor.

28. Official Address Disclaimer

The Resident shall not use the Premises as his/her registered office, principal place of business, GST registration address, shop or establishment address, correspondence address for any regulated activity, professional office address, or for obtaining any trade licence, statutory registration, tax registration, or business permit, unless expressly permitted by the Licensor in writing.

29. Non-Solicitation of Residents

The Resident shall not solicit, approach, canvass, market, advertise, or induce other residents for commercial activities, business promotion, recruitment, sales, investment schemes, subscriptions, marketing initiatives, financial products, referral chains, or any similar activity without prior written approval of the Licensor.

30. Code of Conduct and Community Standards

The Resident acknowledges that the Premises operate as a shared co-living environment and shall maintain conduct ensuring safety, discipline, hygiene, mutual respect and peaceful co-existence.

The Resident shall not engage in any act, omission or behaviour that disrupts peaceful enjoyment, interferes with operations, causes nuisance or affects the safety, reputation or discipline of the Premises.

31. Photography, Recording and Social Media

The Resident shall not photograph, record, livestream, capture images, or make audio/video recordings of other residents, staff, visitors, restricted areas, security arrangements, internal operations, or private/common areas of the Premises without consent or authorization, except where required by law.

The Resident shall not publish or circulate false, malicious, defamatory, misleading, abusive, or reputation-damaging statements regarding the Premises, Licensor, management, residents, staff, services, facilities, or operations through social media, public platforms, online reviews, messaging groups, or any other medium.

Nothing contained herein shall restrict the Resident from raising genuine grievances through lawful and appropriate channels.

32. Misconduct

Misconduct shall include, without limitation: abusive, threatening, violent, offensive or inappropriate behaviour; illegal activities; possession or consumption of prohibited substances; repeated violation of house rules; nuisance or disturbance; property damage; unauthorized occupancy or guests; harassment; non-compliance with management instructions; and any act affecting safety, reputation, discipline or operations.

33. Consequences of Misconduct

The Licensor may take action including warning, penalty, suspension of services, restriction of access, relocation, termination, eviction without refund, forfeiture of deposit and/or legal action.

The decision of the Licensor in matters of safety, discipline, conduct, relocation and operational control shall be final and binding.

34. Social Living Risks

The Resident acknowledges the inherent risks and practical limitations of shared/community living.

The Licensor shall not be responsible for disputes between residents, interpersonal conflicts, emotional distress, personal disagreements or acts/omissions of other residents.

35. Indemnity

The Resident shall indemnify, defend and hold harmless the Licensor, its directors, officers, employees, representatives and agents from all claims, losses, liabilities, damages, penalties, proceedings and expenses including reasonable legal costs arising from breach of this Agreement, misconduct, violation of law, acts or omissions of the Resident or visitors, third-party disputes, damage to property or breach of policies.

This clause shall survive termination or expiry of this Agreement.

36. Limitation of Liability

The Licensor shall not be liable for acts of other residents, disputes, emotional distress, service interruptions, internet downtime, theft/loss of belongings, indirect losses or force majeure events.

The aggregate liability of the Licensor, if established, shall not exceed the security deposit actually received under this Agreement.

37. Parking Facilities

Parking facilities, if provided, shall be subject to availability, internal policy, allocation by the Licensor, and payment of applicable charges, if any.

Use of parking facilities shall be entirely at the sole risk of the Resident. The Licensor shall not be liable for theft, loss, damage, deterioration, accident, vandalism, fire, flooding, or any damage to vehicles or articles left therein.

The Resident shall park only in designated areas and shall comply with parking instructions issued by the Licensor from time to time.

38. Evidence and Enforcement

CCTV footage, access records, complaint records, digital logs, emails, WhatsApp communications, application records, internal reports and electronic records may be relied upon as evidence.

Electronic records shall be valid and admissible for all purposes relating to enforcement of this Agreement.

39. Data Privacy and Consent

The Resident consents to the collection, storage, processing, verification, and use of personal information, identity documents, employment details, contact details, emergency contact details, access records, CCTV records, payment records, complaint records, and other resident-related information by the Licensor for operational, security, regulatory, verification, statutory compliance, safety, resident management, and dispute resolution purposes.

The Licensor shall process such information subject to applicable laws and may disclose the same to law enforcement authorities, statutory authorities, verification agencies, service providers, legal advisors, or other persons where required for lawful, operational, compliance, or security purposes.

40. Termination and Exit

During the lock-in period, if the Resident exits, vacates, abandons or discontinues stay, the Resident shall be liable to pay rent/license fee for the balance lock-in period together with all applicable charges until such replacement is secured.

If the Resident identifies a suitable replacement acceptable to the Licensor and such replacement executes a fresh agreement and occupies the accommodation, liability shall stand reduced up to the date of onboarding of such replacement. Approval of replacement shall remain at the sole discretion of the Licensor.

After completion of the lock-in period, termination may be effected only by giving thirty (30) days’ prior written notice. Failure to provide such notice shall entitle the Licensor to deduct notice period dues equivalent to thirty (30) days’ rent/license fee from the security deposit and/or recover the same separately.

At the time of exit, the Resident shall clear all outstanding dues, return all access devices/assets, hand over possession peacefully and complete all exit formalities.

Exit shall be subject to joint inspection of the allotted accommodation, verification of inventory and access devices, assessment of outstanding dues, electricity charges, penalties, damages, missing items, and completion of all exit formalities prescribed by the Licensor.

In case of overstay after termination, expiry or notice period, penal unauthorized occupancy charges shall apply on a per-day basis as determined by the Licensor, and the Licensor may restrict services and take lawful steps to recover possession.

The Licensor may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect in case of breach, misconduct, non-payment, illegal activity, safety concern, policy violation or conduct prejudicial to the Premises or its occupants.

41. Force Majeure

The Licensor shall not be liable for delay, interruption or failure caused by events beyond reasonable control including natural calamities, pandemics, government restrictions, utility failure, technical failure, strikes, riots, labour shortage, fire, flood or acts of God.

42. Dispute Resolution and Arbitration

The Parties shall first attempt amicable resolution. Failing amicable resolution, disputes shall be referred to arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.

The arbitral tribunal shall consist of a sole arbitrator mutually appointed by the Parties. In case of failure to agree within fifteen (15) days, the arbitrator shall be appointed in accordance with the Act.

The seat and venue of arbitration shall be Ahmedabad and the language shall be English.

43. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Agreement shall be governed by Indian law. Subject to the arbitration clause, courts at Ahmedabad alone shall have exclusive jurisdiction, notwithstanding the place of execution or residence of the Resident.

44. Communication

All notices, approvals, consents and communications may be sent to the Resident through RPAD, courier, email, mobile application, WhatsApp or other recognized digital platforms using the details specified in Annexure A.

Electronic communications shall be deemed received upon successful transmission unless a delivery failure notification is received.

45. Digital Execution and Electronic Acceptance

This Agreement may be executed physically or electronically, including through digital signatures, OTP-based acceptance, mobile application acknowledgement, email confirmation, scanned signatures, e-sign platforms, click-wrap acceptance, or any other legally recognised electronic means.

Each such mode shall constitute valid execution and acceptance of this Agreement and shall be binding on the Parties.

The Parties agree that electronic records, digital acknowledgements, and electronically executed versions of this Agreement shall have the same legal effect as a physically signed document.

46. Amendments

The Licensor may amend operational policies, house rules, guidelines and procedures upon reasonable prior notice. However, amendments to core commercial terms shall require written consent, unless otherwise permitted under this Agreement.

47. Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with its annexures and documents incorporated by reference, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior discussions, representations and understandings.

48. Severability

If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

49. Waiver

No failure or delay in enforcing any right shall constitute waiver. Any waiver must be in writing and shall apply only to the specific instance for which it is granted.

50. Survival

Clauses relating to dues, damages, indemnity, limitation of liability, dispute resolution, jurisdiction, evidence and accrued rights shall survive termination or expiry.

51. Binding Effect

This Agreement shall bind the Parties and their respective successors, legal representatives, heirs and permitted assigns.

52. No Tenancy / Exclusive Possession Disclaimer

The Resident expressly acknowledges that no tenancy, lease, sub-tenancy, exclusive possession or proprietary right is created under this Agreement.

The accommodation forms part of a managed co-living facility with bundled services, amenities and shared infrastructure. Occupancy is incidental to the provision of such managed services.

The Resident shall not claim protection under rent control laws or any statute applicable to tenants or lessees.

53. Declaration

The Resident declares that all information furnished in the annexures is true and correct, that he/she has read and understood this Agreement, and that he/she voluntarily executes this Agreement.

Annexure E – Do’s and Don’ts

The following Do’s and Don’ts are illustrative and not exhaustive. The Resident shall comply with the same, along with all other house rules, operational policies, safety instructions, circulars, notices, and directions issued by the Licensor from time to time. Any violation thereof may be treated as breach of the Agreement and/or misconduct, as determined by the Licensor.

Do’s
  1. Maintain cleanliness and hygiene in the allotted room, bed space, washrooms, and common areas.
  2. Pay rent, electricity charges, penalties, and other dues within the prescribed timelines.
  3. Use access cards, keys, biometric access, QR codes, and mobile passes responsibly and only for personal access.
  4. Intimate the Licensor/warden regarding late entry, night-outs, prolonged absence, or any emergency.
  5. Immediately report loss of keys, access cards, devices, parcels, or personal belongings.
  6. Raise complaints and service requests only through the designated application or prescribed communication channel.
  7. Cooperate during inspection, maintenance, police verification, statutory verification, and exit formalities.
  8. Use Wi-Fi/internet facilities responsibly and only for lawful purposes.
  9. Respect other residents, staff, visitors, and the shared co-living environment.
  10. Dispose of waste, sanitary products, and food items only in designated areas/bins.
  11. Switch off lights, fans, AC, chargers, and electrical appliances before leaving the room.
  12. Collect parcels and deliveries promptly from the designated area.
  13. Keep food items labelled with name and room/bed number.
  14. Report any safety concern, damage, misconduct, suspicious activity, or emergency immediately.
  15. Follow all house rules, operational policies, circulars, and instructions issued by the Licensor.
Don’ts
  1. Do not smoke, vape, consume alcohol, or possess/consume prohibited substances in rooms or common areas.
  2. Do not allow overnight visitors or unauthorized guests.
  3. Do not share, duplicate, transfer, lend, or misuse access cards, keys, biometric credentials, QR codes, or mobile access passes.
  4. Do not damage, alter, tamper with, or misuse furniture, fittings, appliances, fixtures, CCTV, fire safety equipment, or electrical systems.
  5. Do not use electric kettles, induction cooktops, heaters, hot plates, immersion rods, gas stoves, or cooking/heating appliances inside rooms without written permission.
  6. Do not affix posters, stickers, nails, hooks, adhesives, or any material on walls, cupboards, furniture, or fixtures.
  7. Do not create nuisance, disturbance, loud noise, harassment, intimidation, or inconvenience to other residents.
  8. Do not conduct commercial activities, client meetings, business promotion, sales, recruitment, or marketing activities from the Premises without written approval.
  9. Do not use the Premises as a registered office, GST address, business address, correspondence address for regulated activity, or trade licence address.
  10. Do not photograph, record, livestream, or capture images/videos of other residents, staff, visitors, restricted areas, or internal operations without consent.
  11. Do not publish false, defamatory, malicious, misleading, or reputation-damaging content regarding the Premises, management, staff, or residents.
  12. Do not keep valuables, cash, jewellery, or important documents unattended.
  13. Do not flush sanitary pads, food waste, plastic, cloth, or other prohibited materials in toilets.
  14. Do not store expired, leaking, open, spoiled, or unlabelled food in common areas.
  15. Do not obstruct corridors, exits, fire passages, staircases, or common areas.
  16. Do not misuse Wi-Fi/internet for unlawful activity, piracy, hacking, prohibited downloads, cyber offences, or network abuse.
  17. Do not park vehicles outside designated areas.
  18. Do not offer gifts, tips, cash, loans, or favours to staff.
  19. Do not leave belongings behind after exit.
  20. Do not violate any instruction issued for safety, discipline, hygiene, security, or operational control.
